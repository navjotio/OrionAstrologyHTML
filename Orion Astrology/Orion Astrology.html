About

ORION is the prominent constellation located on the CELESTIAL EQUATOR and visible throughout the world. It is one the most conspicuous and recognizable constellations in the night sky. It is named after ORION, a hunter in GREEK MYTHOLOGY. Its brightest stars are BLUE-WHITE RIGEL and RED BETELGEUSE. The earliset known depiction linked to the constellation of ORION is a prehistoric craving found in a cave in the ACH VALLEY in West Germany in 1979. Archaelogists estimate that is 32,000 to 38,000 YEARS OLD.
